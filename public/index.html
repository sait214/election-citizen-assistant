📚 Voter Education Hub

Empowering citizens with knowledge for a stronger democracy

Voting is not just a right; it's your voice in the decision-making process of the nation. When you vote, you participate in shaping laws, policies, and the future of your community.

  • Civic Duty: It is a fundamental responsibility of every citizen in a democracy.
  • Impact: Many elections have been decided by a handful of votes. Your vote counts!
  • Historical Significance: Generations fought for the universal right to vote; honor that legacy by participating.
1Search Name: Verify your name in the electoral roll via ECI website.
2Find Booth: Locate your assigned polling station.
3ID Check: Show your Voter ID or approved identification at the booth.
4Ink & Slip: Officer marks finger with ink and provides a voter slip.
5Cast Vote: Press the blue button for your candidate on the EVM.
6Verify: Watch the VVPAT screen for 7 seconds to confirm your choice.

Welcome to the democratic process! Use this checklist to ensure you are ready:

  • Right to Secrecy: Your vote is 100% anonymous. No one can force you to reveal it.
  • Right to Information: Know the background and assets of every candidate.
  • NOTA Option: Right to reject all candidates if you find none suitable.
  • Assistance: Right to take a companion if you are blind or infirm.
  • Tendered Vote: Right to vote via paper ballot if someone already voted in your name.
Myth

EVMs can be hacked via WiFi or Bluetooth.

Fact

EVMs are standalone devices with no wireless or internet connectivity.

Myth

If NOTA wins, the election is cancelled.

Fact

Current rules state the candidate with second-highest votes is declared winner.

Stop the spread of misinformation during elections:

Check the Source: Is the news from a verified ECI handle or major news outlet?
Look for Official Logos: Official circulars always have authorized logos and signatures.
Verify via ECI Website: Visit eci.gov.in for all official announcements.
Don't Panic: Rumors about "voting cancelled" or "machine errors" are often fakes.
